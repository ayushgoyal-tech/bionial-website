Manufacturing Overview

Our tablet compression and coating suite handles everything from standard film-coated tablets to complex enteric and extended-release formats. Multi-layer and bi-layer tablet capability for combination products.

Available Formats & Variants

All variants are manufactured under WHO-GMP conditions with full batch records, in-process checks, and final release testing.

Film-Coated Tablets

Standard compression with film coating. Improves appearance, masks taste, and adds moisture protection for vitamins, minerals, and herbal actives.

Enteric-Coated Tablets

Polymer coating that resists gastric acid. Essential for acid-sensitive actives like probiotics, enzymes, and delayed-release formulations.

Extended-Release Tablets

Matrix or reservoir-based extended release for once-daily dosing. Ideal for minerals, melatonin, and high-dose vitamins.

Effervescent Tablets

Rapid dissolution in water. Superior absorption and patient compliance for vitamins C, D, B-complex, and electrolytes.

Sublingual / Buccal Tablets

Fast disintegration under the tongue for rapid systemic onset — B12, melatonin, and herbal tincture formats.

Bi-layer Tablets

Two distinct layers in one tablet — enabling combination actives with different release profiles or incompatible ingredients.

How It Works

From inquiry to dispatch in 30 days — our streamlined manufacturing process keeps your project on track at every stage.

01

Consultation & Brief

Share your product requirements — target audience, dosage, packaging preferences, and budget. We'll provide a feasibility assessment and cost estimate within 48 hours.

02

Formulation & Sampling

Our R&D team develops or adapts the formulation. Bench samples are prepared for your approval — colour, texture, dissolution, and taste sign-off.

03

Batch Production

On sample approval, we move to full batch production. Complete batch manufacturing records, in-process checks, and traceability throughout.

04

Final product undergoes complete quality testing before release — assay, dissolution, microbiology, and stability. COA and packaging dispatch within the agreed timeline.
